The Path to Becoming a Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA)

Are you passionate about healthcare and interested in starting a career as a Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA)? Unlike most nursing and medical jobs, CNAs don't need to earn a university or college degree. However, obtaining a certificate or diploma in the field is essential to acquire the necessary skills for personal patient care, communication, vital signs monitoring, mental health support, and more.

In Kenya, the demand for nursing assistants is high, making it an opportune time to pursue a CNA course. But where can you find such courses, and what are the admission requirements?

Responsibilities of a Certified Nursing Assistant

As a certified nursing assistant, you'll play a vital role in the healthcare team. Some of your major responsibilities include:

  • Gathering medical supplies
  • Turning and moving patients
  • Taking vital signs
  • Providing companionship
  • Implementing safety procedures
  • Taking care of wounds
  • Grooming patients
  • Assisting with medical procedures
  • Transporting patients
  • Documenting information

Training Structure

Our Nurse Assistant Training classes consist of three components:

  1. Fundamentals: Covering roles and responsibilities, legal issues, ethics, patient communications, anatomy, and physiology.
  2. Skills Lab Practice: Hands-on practice in personal care, patient positioning, transferring, vital signs monitoring, and more.
  3. Clinical Training: Working directly with residents of a long-term care facility under supervision, gaining practical experience.
